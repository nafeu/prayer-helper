# How To Pray

A step-by-step guide to performing Salat (Prayer) in the Sunni-Muslim tradition.

## Step 1: Intention (Niyyah)

**Instructions:** Make your intention in your heart to perform the specific prayer (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, or Isha).

**Arabic:** النية

**Transliteration:** An-Niyyah

**Translation:** The intention

**Why:** The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said, "Actions are by intentions." Making a clear intention helps focus the mind and heart on worship.

---

## Step 2: Takbir (Opening)

**Instructions:** Raise your hands to your ears (or shoulders) and say "Allahu Akbar" (God is Greatest).

**Arabic:** الله أكبر

**Transliteration:** Allahu Akbar

**Translation:** God is Greatest

**Why:** This marks the beginning of prayer and signifies leaving worldly matters behind. The hands raised symbolize submission and readiness to worship.

---

## Step 3: Standing (Qiyam) - Recitation

**Instructions:** Place your right hand over your left hand on your chest. Recite Surah Al-Fatiha, followed by another surah or verses from the Quran.

**Arabic:** 
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

**Transliteration:** 
Bismillahir Rahmanir Raheem
Alhamdu lillahi rabbil alameen
Ar-Rahmanir Raheem
Maliki yawmid deen
Iyyaka na'budu wa iyyaka nasta'een
Ihdinas siratal mustaqeem
Siratal latheena an'amta alayhim ghayril maghdoobi alayhim wa lad daleen

**Translation:** 
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful
Praise be to Allah, the Lord of all the worlds
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful
Master of the Day of Judgment
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help
Guide us to the straight path
The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have evoked anger or of those who are astray

**Why:** Surah Al-Fatiha is the opening chapter of the Quran and is considered the essence of the entire book. It must be recited in every rakat of prayer. It establishes the relationship between the worshipper and Allah.

---

## Step 4: Ruku (Bowing)

**Instructions:** Say "Allahu Akbar" and bow down, placing your hands on your knees. Keep your back straight and say the following three times.

**Arabic:** سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيمِ

**Transliteration:** Subhana rabbiyal azeem

**Translation:** Glory be to my Lord, the Great

**Why:** Bowing demonstrates humility and submission to Allah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said, "The closest a servant is to his Lord is when he is in prostration."

---

## Step 5: Standing from Ruku

**Instructions:** Rise from bowing while saying the following, then stand straight.

**Arabic:** سَمِعَ اللَّهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ

**Transliteration:** Sami'Allahu liman hamidah

**Translation:** Allah hears those who praise Him

**Then say:** رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ

**Transliteration:** Rabbana wa lakal hamd

**Translation:** Our Lord, to You is all praise

**Why:** This acknowledges that Allah hears our praise and we give thanks for His blessings.

---

## Step 6: Sujud (Prostration)

**Instructions:** Say "Allahu Akbar" and prostrate, placing your forehead, nose, both hands, knees, and toes on the ground. Say the following three times.

**Arabic:** سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى

**Transliteration:** Subhana rabbiyal a'la

**Translation:** Glory be to my Lord, the Most High

**Why:** Prostration is the highest form of submission in prayer. The Prophet (peace be upon him) was commanded to prostrate, and it brings the worshipper closest to Allah.

---

## Step 7: Sitting Between Prostrations

**Instructions:** Say "Allahu Akbar" and sit up briefly, then prostrate again.

**Arabic:** (While sitting) رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي

**Transliteration:** Rabbi ghfir li

**Translation:** My Lord, forgive me

**Why:** This brief pause between prostrations allows for reflection and seeking forgiveness.

---

## Step 8: Second Sujud

**Instructions:** Perform the second prostration exactly like the first, saying "Subhana rabbiyal a'la" three times.

**Why:** Two prostrations complete one rakat. This is based on the practice of the Prophet (peace be upon him).

---

## Step 9: Tashahhud (Sitting for Testimony)

**Instructions:** After completing the required number of rakats, sit and recite the Tashahhud.

**Arabic:** 
التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَى عِبَادِ اللَّهِ الصَّالِحِينَ، أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

**Transliteration:** 
At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibatu. As-salamu 'alayka ayyuhan-nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuhu. As-salamu 'alayna wa 'ala 'ibadillahis-salihin. Ashhadu an la ilaha illallah wa ashhadu anna Muhammadan 'abduhu wa rasuluhu.

**Translation:** 
All greetings, prayers, and good words are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy of Allah and His blessings. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and messenger.

**Why:** This is the testimony of faith (Shahada) and acknowledgment of the Prophet's message. It's recited in the final sitting of every prayer.

---

## Step 10: Salawat (Blessings on the Prophet)

**Instructions:** After Tashahhud, send blessings upon the Prophet Muhammad.

**Arabic:** 
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ

**Transliteration:** 
Allahumma salli 'ala Muhammadin wa 'ala ali Muhammadin, kama sallayta 'ala Ibrahima wa 'ala ali Ibrahima, innaka hamidun majid.

**Translation:** 
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and the family of Muhammad, as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed, You are Praiseworthy and Glorious.

**Why:** This is a command from Allah in the Quran (33:56) to send blessings upon the Prophet. It's a way to show love and respect for the final messenger.

---

## Step 11: Final Salam (Ending)

**Instructions:** Turn your head to the right and say "Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah" (Peace be upon you and the mercy of Allah), then turn to the left and repeat.

**Arabic:** السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ

**Transliteration:** Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah

**Translation:** Peace be upon you and the mercy of Allah

**Why:** The prayer ends with greetings of peace, symbolizing that you're returning to the world and greeting the angels who record your deeds. This completes the prayer.

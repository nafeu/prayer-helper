# Pre-Prayer Quiz

Test your knowledge about prayer rituals and Quranic verses.

## Question 1

**What must be recited in every rakat of prayer?**

- [ ] Surah Al-Ikhlas
- [x] Surah Al-Fatiha
- [ ] Surah An-Nas
- [ ] Any surah from the Quran

**Explanation:** Surah Al-Fatiha is mandatory in every rakat of every prayer. It's called "The Mother of the Book" and is essential to the validity of prayer.

---

## Question 2

**What do you say during Ruku (bowing)?**

- [ ] Subhana rabbiyal a'la
- [x] Subhana rabbiyal azeem
- [ ] Allahu Akbar
- [ ] Sami'Allahu liman hamidah

**Explanation:** During Ruku, you say "Subhana rabbiyal azeem" (Glory be to my Lord, the Great) three times. "Subhana rabbiyal a'la" is said during prostration.

---

## Question 3

**What is the meaning of "Iyyaka na'budu wa iyyaka nasta'een" from Surah Al-Fatiha?**

- [ ] Guide us to the straight path
- [x] You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help
- [ ] Praise be to Allah, the Lord of all the worlds
- [ ] Master of the Day of Judgment

**Explanation:** This verse establishes that worship and seeking help belong exclusively to Allah, emphasizing Tawheed (oneness of Allah).

---

## Question 4

**How many prostrations (sujud) are in one complete rakat?**

- [ ] One
- [x] Two
- [ ] Three
- [ ] Four

**Explanation:** Each rakat consists of two prostrations (sujud). After the first prostration, you sit briefly, then perform the second prostration.

---

## Question 5

**What surah is equivalent to one-third of the Quran in reward?**

- [ ] Surah Al-Fatiha
- [x] Surah Al-Ikhlas
- [ ] Surah An-Nas
- [ ] Surah Al-Kawthar

**Explanation:** The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that reciting Surah Al-Ikhlas is equivalent to reciting one-third of the Quran in reward.

---

## Question 6

**What do you say when rising from Ruku?**

- [ ] Subhana rabbiyal azeem
- [x] Sami'Allahu liman hamidah, then Rabbana wa lakal hamd
- [ ] Allahu Akbar
- [ ] Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah

**Explanation:** When rising from Ruku, you say "Sami'Allahu liman hamidah" (Allah hears those who praise Him), then "Rabbana wa lakal hamd" (Our Lord, to You is all praise).

---

## Question 7

**What is recited during the final sitting (Tashahhud) of prayer?**

- [ ] Only Surah Al-Fatiha
- [x] The testimony of faith (Shahada) and blessings on the Prophet
- [ ] Only blessings on the Prophet
- [ ] Any dua (supplication)

**Explanation:** The Tashahhud includes the testimony of faith (Shahada) acknowledging that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is His messenger, followed by blessings on the Prophet.

---

## Question 8

**What is the significance of Surah Al-Falaq and An-Nas?**

- [ ] They are the longest surahs
- [x] They are known as "Al-Mu'awwidhatayn" (the two protectors) and are recited for protection
- [ ] They must be recited in every prayer
- [ ] They are only for special occasions

**Explanation:** These two surahs are called "Al-Mu'awwidhatayn" and are recited for seeking protection from evil. The Prophet (peace be upon him) recited them every night.

---

## Question 9

**How do you end the prayer?**

- [ ] By saying "Allahu Akbar"
- [x] By turning your head right and left saying "Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah"
- [ ] By standing up
- [ ] By reciting Surah Al-Fatiha again

**Explanation:** The prayer ends with the final Salam, turning your head to the right and left, saying "Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah" (Peace be upon you and the mercy of Allah).

---

## Question 10

**What is the meaning of "Qul huwa Allahu ahad" from Surah Al-Ikhlas?**

- [ ] Say, I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind
- [x] Say, "He is Allah, [who is] One"
- [ ] Say, Praise be to Allah
- [ ] Say, Allah is the Greatest

**Explanation:** This is the opening verse of Surah Al-Ikhlas, emphasizing the absolute oneness of Allah (Tawheed).

---

## Question 11

**What do you say during Sujud (prostration)?**

- [ ] Subhana rabbiyal azeem
- [x] Subhana rabbiyal a'la
- [ ] Sami'Allahu liman hamidah
- [ ] Rabbana wa lakal hamd

**Explanation:** During prostration, you say "Subhana rabbiyal a'la" (Glory be to my Lord, the Most High) three times.

---

## Question 12

**What is the significance of raising hands and saying "Allahu Akbar" at the beginning of prayer?**

- [ ] It's optional
- [x] It marks the beginning of prayer and signifies leaving worldly matters behind
- [ ] It's only for the first rakat
- [ ] It's a cultural practice

**Explanation:** The opening Takbir (Allahu Akbar) with raised hands marks the formal beginning of prayer and symbolizes entering into a state of worship, leaving worldly concerns behind.
